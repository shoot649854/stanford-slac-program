What's neutrino

It has 3 types of nutrino, electron neutrino (νe) muon neutrino (νμ) tau neutrino (ντ). Nutrino does not react for most of the time, like this electron goes through without hitting. Nutrino was not discovered before 1990, but based on the study, scientists found out that Nutrino exists a lot more in our planet. It comes from the sun as solar nutrino, from planet explosion as nuclear reactor, and from atmosphere as atmospheric nutrino.

Solar Neutrino

Solar neutrinos are electron neutrinos produced in the sun as a result of nuclear fusion. This reaction produces 86% of all solar neutrinos. In the proton-proton chain reaction in the standard solar model, deuterium fuses with another proton to form three helium atoms and gamma rays.

Atmospheric Neutrino

Atmospheric neutrinos are typically produced around 15 kilometers above Earth’s surface. They form when a cosmic ray, an energetic particle from space, crashes into Earth’s atmosphere. These particles are typically protons, though they can also be helium or heavier nuclei. When they strike an atomic nucleus in our atmosphere, there is a cascade of particles. Short-lived particles called mesons form, most of them pions (disappointingly, they are not made of pie). These are unstable particles made of two quarks, and they rapidly decay into muons and muon antineutrinos (or antimuons and muon neutrinos). A muon is also unstable, so it will usually decay into an electron, electron antineutrino, and muon neutrino. Thus, about two-thirds of atmospheric neutrinos are muon neutrinos and antineutrinos, and the remainder are electron neutrinos and antineutrinos.

The Liquid Argon Time Projection Chamber (LArTPC)

Calorimetric information is important for particle identification, which is the basis for neutrino profile and oscillation measurements, an exploration beyond standard model physics. One of the major challenges in the adoption of LArTPC technology is to characterize its performance and quantify the associated systematic uncertainties. MicroBooNE, the longest operating LArTPC to date, has performed a large number of such measurements, including detector physics and electromagnetic shower reconstruction studies.

Neutrino Oscillation

Standard model neutrinos have no strong or electromagnetic interactions, only weak and gravitational interactions. However, because of its very small mass, it reacts very little with gravitational interactions, so it reacts only slightly with other elementary particles and is highly permeable.Therefore, observation using collisions with atomic nuclei and electrons is difficult, and it is necessary to prepare highly sensitive sensors and neutrino detectors composed of large-mass reactive materials in order to capture reactions that are extremely rare. Progress in research has been slow compared to particles. Some theories postulate that the neutrinos after the fourth generation are sterile neutrinos that only interact with gravity, but their existence has not yet been confirmed experimentally.

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is an important way of thinking in studying the movement of neutrinos. In order to understand the phenomena of atoms, molecules, and light, new laws of motion were discovered to replace classical theories such as Newton's laws of motion and Maxwell's laws of electromagnetics, and they became a system of mechanics. This is quantum mechanics.

Quantum Mechanics of Neutrino Oscillations

Because neutrinos do not readily react with other elementary particles, even the basic property of mass cannot be measured. Since its discovery, neutrinos have been thought to have nearly zero mass, and for many years they have been positioned as elementary particles with zero mass in the Standard Model. "Neutrino oscillation" is a phenomenon in which the "type" changes, such as one type of neutrino changing into another type of neutrino during flight, and then changing back to the original type of neutrino. By observing electrons, muons, and tau particles that are paired when a neutrino reacts, and determining the type of neutrino, we can understand the type change.
